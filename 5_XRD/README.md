# X-Ray Diffraction 

## Principle
- It ensures how atoms are arranged in a material. It is based on the elastic scattering of X-ray by atoms in a crystal lattice.
- ##### It provides crucial information about
  - Crystal Structures and phase identification with high precision.
  - Lattice parameters and unit cell dimensions for structural characteristics.

- ##### Crystalline Material
  - The materials must have long range periodic atomic arrangement to produce sharp diffraction peaks.
  - Amorphous materials produce broad peaks.
 
- ##### Monochromatic Radiation
  - X-ray beam must be monochromatic (single wavelength) to enable accurate d-spacing calculations.
    - ###### d-spacing
      - D-spacing is the distance between adjacent planes of atoms in a crystal lattice, crucial for analyzing crystal structures using X-ray diffraction.
